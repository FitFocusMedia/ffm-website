The FFM Difference

Why Fit Focus Media

IBC is not just looking for a camera crew. You're looking for a partner who can transform Challenger Series fighters into stars, turn fight nights into must-watch events, and build a content engine that keeps audiences engaged between shows. That's what FFM was built for.

🎬 Cinematic Production Quality

FFM shoots on cinema cameras — not consumer DSLRs or phone rigs. Every punch, every reaction, every walkout is captured with the visual language of a feature film. Multiple cameras stationed around the ring at all times ensure no angle is missed, feeding both the live stream and a dedicated social content pipeline simultaneously.

🤖 AI-Powered Content at Scale

FFM turns events into content ecosystems. AI-driven automation identifies the most engaging moments in real time and auto-generates vertical clips for TikTok, Reels, and Shorts while the event is still running. Automated distribution pushes content across all platforms with optimised formatting, and generative media creates dynamic promotional material and fighter profiles that evolve as the series progresses.

📖 Storytelling That Builds Stars

FFM's approach is cinematic and narrative-driven. We don't just film fights — we create storylines. Viewers don't tune in for anonymous fighters; they tune in for characters they care about. Pre-fight profile packages, in-event rivalries, and post-fight arcs carry momentum into the next show and, ultimately, their IBC debut. Engaged viewers become loyal fans, loyal fans become ticket buyers, and ticket buyers become the audience that makes Challenger Series commercially viable.

🌍 Proven Track Record

FFM has delivered live production, content creation, and social management across multiple sports — including dedicated combat sports coverage. We've produced events across Australia and internationally, with the technical experience and on-ground expertise to execute flawlessly under pressure.

🚀 The Next Level

FFM wants to showcase a side of combat sports that hasn't been seen before. We love this sport, we understand its culture, and we have the tools, team, and vision to elevate Challenger Series beyond a standard fight night into a brand that audiences follow, fighters aspire to, and sponsors want to be part of.

💪 Skin in the Game

This is a 50/50 project split, which means FFM is investing upfront — not just financially, but with time, expertise, equipment, and relationships built over years in the industry.

Full production kit: Cinema cameras, multi-camera rigs, lighting, audio, and streaming infrastructure — not rented, not borrowed. Owned, maintained, and constantly upgraded.

Years of experience: FFM has delivered live combat sports coverage across Australia and internationally. We've made the mistakes, learned the lessons, and built systems that work under pressure.

Consistent reinvestment: FFM continually reinvests revenue into better equipment, new technology, and training. This isn't static — it's a constantly improving production capability.

Dedicated time: Large portions of FFM's direct time will go into getting Challenger Series off the ground. This isn't an after-hours project squeezed between other clients. It's a priority.

Recognition and growth: FFM will actively present Challenger Series to networks, sponsors, and industry contacts to accelerate growth. We have relationships with brands, platforms, and people who can move the needle.

Leveraging connections: FFM will bring on other high-level skilled people — editors, motion designers, social media strategists, and industry specialists — as the project scales. Our network becomes Challenger Series' network.

We're not billing hourly and walking away. We're not a vendor. We're partners with real skin in the game. When Challenger Series wins, FFM wins. When IBC grows, FFM grows. That's the only structure that creates the incentive to keep pushing, keep improving, and keep building long after the first show.

Term & Long-Term Vision

A Partnership Built to Last

This is not a short-term experiment — it is a long-term commitment to building the IBC Challenger Series into a full-fledged combat sports property and, ultimately, a cornerstone of the IBC brand.

Initial Term

12 months from contract execution. Automatic 12-month renewal unless 90 days' written notice given by either party.

Long-Term Vision

Both parties view this partnership through a multi-year lens:

12 Months

Establish the Challenger Series foundation, build audience, prove the model.

18 Months

Scale audience and sponsorships, expand fighter roster, introduce recurring programming.

24 Months

Challenger Series becomes a recognised brand in Australian combat sports.

36–48 Months

Challenger Series matures into a self-sustaining property with international reach, feeding a pipeline of fighters into IBC main events and potentially becoming a standalone broadcast product.

FFM's commitment extends beyond the initial term. We are not looking for a one-year side project — we are looking to build something that grows in value, audience, and revenue for years to come. Every piece of content, every fighter profile, every storyline is an investment in long-term brand equity for both IBC and FFM.

Phase 1 Commitment

Minimum: 3 Challenger Series events (Phase 1) + house launch (Phase 2, subject to performance benchmarks). Phase 2 activation is contingent on meeting benchmarks, but both parties commit to good-faith discussion and strategy adjustment if benchmarks are narrowly missed.
